# Sunfruit Formulation & Product Development Context

## Product Overview

Sunfruit is an organic powdered beverage brand creating sugar-free, preservative-free water enhancers made from real fruits and botanicals. Each flavor pairs one organic fruit with one organic botanical.

## Mixability & Solubility Challenges

### The Core Problem
When powder is placed in water, it clumps and doesn't mix well with a spoon. Vigorous shaking or electric mixing achieves satisfactory solubility with no grittiness. The issue is **wettability** (surface tension/water penetration), not true insolubility.

### Why Fine Particles Make It Worse
- Particles under 100 microns have dramatically increased surface area
- More surface area = stronger surface tension effects
- Fine powders create an impermeable barrier when hitting water
- **Optimal particle size for instant dispersion: 200-500 microns**

### Solution Approaches

**Formulation Solutions:**
1. **Sunflower Lecithin (1.5-2%)** - Natural emulsifier/dispersant that improves wettability
2. **Increase Acacia Fiber** - Has natural emulsifying and dispersing properties
3. **Add Inulin** - Excellent cold-water solubility
4. **Re-evaluate Rice Hull Powder** - May be contributing to hydrophobic issues
5. **Add Citric Acid** - Improves wettability and breaks surface tension

**Process Solutions:**
1. **Agglomeration/Instantization (Most Effective)** - Creates porous 200-500 micron particle clusters that water penetrates easily
2. **Spray Drying** - Can create instant-dissolving powders with 18-24 month shelf life
3. **Particle Size Optimization** - Target 200-500 microns, avoid <100 microns

### Current Reality Check
Competitors like Mud/Wtr and Ryze also have mixability issues with dry-blended powders and rely on frothers. Perfect instant mixing typically requires spray drying with agglomeration.

## Spray Drying vs. Dry Blending

### Cost Comparison
- Spray drying adds **30-100%** to ingredient costs
- At scale (5M+ units), spray-dried ingredients cost approximately **$0.47/serving** vs **$0.10-0.15** for dry-blended

### Spray Drying Benefits
- Creates porous, instantly dispersible particles
- Better flavor encapsulation and protection
- Extended shelf life (18-24 months)
- Premium positioning justification

### Spray Drying Drawbacks
- Significantly higher COGS
- Requires specialized co-packers
- Higher minimum order quantities
- Longer lead times

### Key Decision Point
If maintaining organic + spray-dried + 5g serving, minimum COGS is approximately **$0.45-0.50** per stick. To hit $0.20-0.25 target would require sacrificing organic certification OR reducing serving size to 3-3.5g.

## Formulation Development History

### Blue Pacific Flavors Partnership
- **Services**: Formulation of 5 flavors within 30 hours at $150/hour
- **Timeline**: 8-12 weeks development, 4-6 iterations per flavor
- **Ownership**: Sunfruit retains formulation IP
- **Additional support**: Nutrition facts panel, FDA attorney intro, co-packer introductions

### Iteration Feedback (Key Learnings)

**Round 1 Botanical Powder Versions** - Achieved best flavor depth and balance for Raspberry Hibiscus and Pomegranate Rose

**Key Feedback Points:**
- Lemon Mint: Target lemonade-level tartness, increase citrus, decrease mint
- Grapefruit Ginger: Ginger too spicy/medicinal - botanical needs to COMPLEMENT not FIGHT
- Raspberry Hibiscus: Round 1 powder version was gold standard
- Blueberry Lavender: Lavender diminished too much in later rounds
- Pomegranate Rose: Round 1 powder version was ideal

### Formulation Philosophy
- Sweet fruit + gentle botanical = winning formula
- Assertive fruit + assertive botanical = failure (e.g., Grapefruit Ginger)
- "Would this taste good in spa water?" = good validation test
